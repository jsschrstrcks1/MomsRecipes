She's a Geisha Cocktail

From: Izumi Restaurant

Royal Caribbean Izumi restaurant menu card

Izumi's signature cocktail‚Äîrefreshing and fruity, it pairs nicely with sweet and spicy signature rolls.

Yield 1 cocktail
Prep Time 5 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 oz sake
  • 1/2 oz cranberry juice
  • 3/4 oz lime cordial
  • 6 blueberries , fresh

Instructions

  1. Muddle the blueberries and juices with a spoon or pestle.
  2. Pour sake over ice in a cocktail tin and shake it all together.
  3. Pour the contents into a glass (do not strain) and enjoy.

Notes

  • Recipe from Royal Caribbean cruise ship Izumi restaurant
  • Lime cordial (like Rose's) provides sweetness - fresh lime juice with simple syrup can substitute

Ginger-Onion Lo Mein

Magazine clipping

A savory Chinese noodle dish with fresh ginger, green onions, and oyster sauce.

Yield 4 servings
Prep 15 min
Cook 10 min
Total 25 min

Ingredients

  • 2 bundles fresh Chinese noodles , see notes
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger , cut into fine matchsticks
  • 1/2 tsp Chinese cooking wine , see notes
  • 1/4 cup chicken broth
  • 2 tsp oyster sauce
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 1 pinch white pepper
  • 1 larger pinch sugar
  • 2-3 drops sesame oil
  • 1/4 bunch green onions , cut into 1-inch lengths
  • to taste cilantro , for garnish

Instructions

  1. Bring a pot of water to a rolling boil, add noodles and cook 1 or 2 minutes. Pasta should be al dente.
  2. Drain and rinse with cool water, then drain well.
  3. Heat oil in wok, then add ginger, the cooking wine and chicken broth, stirring all the time.
  4. Next stir in the oyster sauce, salt, white pepper, sugar, and sesame oil.
  5. Add the drained noodles to the wok and toss to coat evenly with the sauce.
  6. Add green onions and toss briefly until just wilted.
  7. Transfer to serving plates and garnish with fresh cilantro. Serve immediately.

Notes

  • Fresh Chinese noodles (lo mein noodles) are available in the refrigerated section of Asian grocery stores. Dried lo mein or egg noodles may be substituted.
  • Chinese cooking wine (Shaoxing wine) can be substituted with dry sherry.
  • Steps 5-7 are inferred from standard lo mein technique as the original clipping was cut off.

Glazed Carrots

The Tampa Tribune, 1994

Simple, healthy glazed carrots with dill and a touch of brown sugar.

Yield 4 servings
Prep 10 min
Cook 8 min
Total 18 min

Ingredients

  • 3 large carrots , peeled and sliced 1/4 inch thick
  • 3/4 cup chicken broth , low sodium
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1/2 tsp dried dill
  • 1/8 tsp white pepper
  • 1/2 tsp lemon juice

Instructions

  1. Place carrots in skillet.
  2. Mix broth, sugar, dill and white pepper; pour over carrots.
  3. Bring to a boil. Cook, uncovered, until liquid has evaporated and carrots are tender, about 8 minutes.
  4. Stir in lemon juice and serve.

Notes

  • Nutrition per serving: Calories 44, Carbohydrates 9g, Protein 2g, Cholesterol 0, Fat 0g, Sodium 167mg

Original Chex Party Mix

From: General Mills

Chex cereal box

The classic savory snack mix with Chex cereals, nuts, pretzels, and a buttery seasoned coating.

Yield 24 servings
Prep 15 min
Total 15-75 min
Temp 250¬∞F

Ingredients

  • 3 cups Corn Chex cereal
  • 3 cups Rice Chex cereal
  • 3 cups Wheat Chex cereal
  • 1 cup mixed nuts
  • 1 cup bite-size pretzels
  • 1 cup garlic-flavor bagel chips , broken into 1-inch pieces
  • 6 tbsp butter or margarine
  • 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 1/2 tsp seasoned salt
  • 3/4 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder

Microwave Instructions

  1. In large microwavable bowl, mix cereals, nuts, pretzels and bagel chips; set aside.
  2. In small microwavable bowl, microwave butter uncovered on High about 40 seconds or until melted. Stir in seasonings.
  3. Pour seasoned butter over cereal mixture; stir until evenly coated.
  4. Microwave uncovered on High 5 to 6 minutes, thoroughly stirring every 2 minutes.
  5. Spread on paper towels to cool. Store in airtight container.

Oven Directions (Alternative)

  1. Pre-heat oven to 250¬∞F (120¬∞C).
  2. Put cereal mixture and seasoning mixture into ungreased roasting pan.
  3. Bake for 1 hour, stirring every 15 minutes.
  4. Spread on paper towels to cool, about 15 minutes. Store in airtight container.

Jubilie Jumbles

From: Carnation / Betty Crocker

Typed recipe card (family collection) - Original recipe from Betty Crocker's Cooky Carnival booklet, circa 1955

Soft, cake-like cookies with walnuts, topped with a rich burnt butter glaze. A classic Carnation Evaporated Milk recipe.

Yield 4 dozen
Prep 20 min
Cook 10 min
Temp 375¬∞F

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup soft shortening
  • 1 cup brown sugar , packed
  • 1/2 cup white sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup evaporated milk , undiluted
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 2 3/4 cups all-purpose flour , sifted
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup walnuts , cut up

Instructions

  1. Combine shortening, sugars, and eggs thoroughly.
  2. Stir in evaporated milk and vanilla.
  3. Sift flour with soda and salt, then stir into mixture.
  4. Blend in nuts.
  5. Refrigerate dough for 1 hour.
  6. Heat oven to 375¬∞F (190¬∞C).
  7. Drop rounded tablespoons of dough 2 inches apart on greased baking sheet.
  8. Bake about 10 minutes, until delicately browned.
  9. While warm, frost with Burnt Butter Glaze. Garnish with walnut halves if desired.

Burnt Butter Glaze

Ingredients: 2 tbsp butter, 2 cups confectioners' sugar (sifted), 1/4 cup evaporated milk (undiluted)

Instructions: Heat butter in small saucepan over medium heat until golden brown, watching carefully to prevent burning. Pour browned butter into bowl. Beat in confectioners' sugar and evaporated milk until smooth and spreadable.

Notes

  • Original recipe card listed "2 tsp" butter for glaze, but canonical Carnation recipe uses 2 tablespoons. Using 2 tbsp as this produces the correct consistency.
  • The original card spelled it "Jubilie" - this may be a regional/family spelling variation of "Jubilee".
